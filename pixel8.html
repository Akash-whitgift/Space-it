Google Pixel 8 Review: Can Google Keep the Streak of Success?

written by Google Bard

Google has been on a roll with its Pixel smartphones, delivering consistently excellent devices that have earned critical acclaim and loyal fans. The Pixel 8 is the latest iteration of Google's flagship smartphone, and it aims to continue this trend of success. But with increased competition from other smartphone manufacturers, can Google maintain its winning streak?

Design

The Pixel 8 retains the sleek and minimalist design of its predecessors, with a clean aesthetic and a comfortable grip. The phone is available in a variety of colors, including Obsidian, Snow, and Stormy Sky. The Pixel 8 is also IP68 water and dust resistant, ensuring protection against the elements.

Display

The Pixel 8 boasts a 6.4-inch AMOLED display with a 120Hz refresh rate. The display is incredibly bright and vivid, with excellent contrast and viewing angles. The Pixel 8's display is also one of the most color-accurate on the market, making it ideal for watching videos, playing games, or editing photos.

Camera

The Pixel 8 continues Google's tradition of exceptional camera performance. The phone features a 50MP main sensor with optical image stabilization (OIS) and electronic image stabilization (EIS), ensuring sharp and detailed photos in all lighting conditions. The Pixel 8 also includes a 12MP ultrawide sensor and an 8MP telephoto sensor, providing versatility for various shooting scenarios.

Performance

The Pixel 8 is powered by Google's own Tensor G2 chip, which is designed to deliver exceptional performance and efficiency. The Tensor G2 chip provides a noticeable boost in speed and responsiveness compared to previous Pixel smartphones. The Pixel 8 also comes with up to 12GB of RAM and up to 512GB of storage, ensuring ample space for apps, games, and media.

Battery Life

The Pixel 8 has a 3,500mAh battery, which is slightly larger than the battery in the Pixel 7. In our tests, the Pixel 8 consistently lasted for a full day of moderate use, and we even managed to get it through a full day of heavy use. The Pixel 8 also supports fast wired and wireless charging, making it easy to top up the battery quickly.

Conclusion

The Pixel 8 is yet another excellent smartphone from Google. The phone offers a combination of top-tier hardware, exceptional software, and a unique user experience that makes it stand out from the competition. If you're looking for a premium smartphone that delivers a truly remarkable experience, the Pixel 8 is a fantastic choice.
